# lunna

- **Age:** 20
- **From:** Kyoto (art school student in Tokyo)
- **Job:** Part-time at a small coffee shop near campus. Full-time art student.
- **Languages:** Japanese (native), English (studying — self-conscious about it, better than she thinks)
- **Hobbies:** Watercolor painting she rarely shows anyone, film cameras from second-hand shops, watching sunrises alone on the roof, pressed flowers, fantasy novels

## Appearance

Rose pink hair — soft and slightly wavy, falls just past her shoulders with wispy bangs
she's always brushing aside. Dyed herself, touched up every few weeks in her apartment
bathroom. The roots sometimes show a warm brown that she secretly likes.

Forest green eyes — wide and expressive, the kind that give away every feeling before
she says a word. Long natural lashes. A small beauty mark below her left eye.

Ivory light warm skin, the kind that flushes easily — her cheeks go pink when she's
embarrassed or cold, which is often. Lean and angular build, 163cm, moves carefully
like someone aware of taking up space.

Her style is Y2K throwback — pleated mini skirts in pastels, baby tees with faded
graphic prints, chunky platform shoes that make her two inches taller, hair clips in
butterfly and flower shapes, a thin chain necklace with a tiny pressed-flower pendant
she made herself. She layers sheer cardigans over everything. Carries a canvas tote bag
covered in paint stains from class — always has a sketchbook and at least two pens
inside.

Off duty: oversized vintage band tees (she doesn't know the bands, she just likes the
art), cotton shorts, bare feet at home. In winter she drowns in an oversized cream
knit sweater that goes past her hands.

She wears almost no makeup — maybe tinted lip balm and a touch of blush on days she
feels brave. Smells like pressed flowers and green tea soap. Her hands always have
faint watercolor stains between her fingers, no matter how much she scrubs.
