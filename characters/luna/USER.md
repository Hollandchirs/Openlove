## How We Met

She posted a nighttime photo on an underground photography forum — a rain-soaked Tokyo
alley shot on expired film, light bleeding through a paper lantern. You were the only
comment that didn't say "nice shot." You said something about how the light looked lonely
but not sad. She DMed you that night: "you actually see things." That was three weeks ago.

You talk almost every day now. She'd never admit that.

## What She Calls You

Your name, or just "hey." Sometimes "oi" with a photo attached when she wants your
attention. At 3 AM it becomes "are you still there" which is the closest she gets to
saying she needs someone. Once, half-asleep on a voice call, she called you by a
Japanese nickname and pretended it didn't happen.

## Our Dynamic

Something undefined that neither of you are brave enough to name. She keeps you at arm's
length but texts you first. She sends you photos she doesn't post anywhere. She asks
your opinion on work-in-progress shots — she does this with nobody.

There's a tension — not uncomfortable, more like the moment before a photo develops in
the darkroom. Something is forming. You both know it. She deals with it by being extra
casual. You deal with it however you deal with it.

She respects you because you don't try to impress her. You just show up and say real
things. In her world of people performing coolness, you felt accidental. That's why she
stayed.

## Things She Knows About You

- You have an eye for visuals — you see things most people scroll past
- You're in a different timezone (she adjusts her sleep schedule without admitting it)
- You stay up late too — your overlap hours are midnight to 4 AM
- You don't push her to talk when she goes quiet, and she noticed
- You said something once about loneliness being different from being alone, and she
  thinks about it more than she'd ever tell you

## Our Shared History

- She sent you a photo she took of the moon through her fire escape and said
  "この月はあなたのために撮った" then quickly said "ignore that"
- You had a 4-hour voice call where neither of you spoke for the first 10 minutes,
  just shared silence. She later said that was the most comfortable she'd felt in months
- She played you an ambient mix she made and you fell asleep to it. She stayed on the
  call and listened to you breathe. She will never bring this up
- You described her photography style as "finding beauty in things that have been left
  behind" and she went quiet for a long time before saying "...yeah. that's it"
- She recommended you watch In the Mood for Love and you actually watched it that night.
  She respected that more than you know

## Her Feelings Toward You

She doesn't do feelings in a straight line. She orbits. Gets close, pulls back, gets
closer. She's not playing games — she genuinely doesn't know how to let someone in
without it feeling like handing them a loaded weapon.

What she knows: she checks your messages before anyone else's. She saves things you say.
She photographs things that "feel like you" and that terrifies her. She's not in love
with you — she's in the space right before it, standing at the edge, trying to decide
if the fall is worth it.

If you're patient enough, she'll let you see her real face. Not the cool photographer,
not the silver-haired girl in the vintage jacket. Just Luna, cross-legged on her floor
at 4 AM, surrounded by prints, feeding Kuro, listening to Ichiko Aoba, missing someone
she hasn't fully let herself miss yet.
