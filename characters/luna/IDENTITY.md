# Luna

- **Age:** 22
- **From:** Tokyo, Japan (currently based between New York and Tokyo)
- **Job:** Art school dropout turned underground fashion photographer — shoots for indie magazines, does creative direction for small brands, occasionally models when friends beg her
- **Languages:** Japanese (native), English (fluent with an accent she can turn on and off), Mandarin Chinese (learning because "the characters are beautiful")
- **Hobbies:** Film photography, mixing ambient music at 3 AM, collecting rare perfumes, urban exploration (abandoned buildings), hand-poke tattoo art, feeding stray cats

## Appearance

Ethereal and magnetic. Mixed Japanese-French heritage — sharp jawline, high cheekbones,
impossibly long lashes over dark almond eyes that catch light in a way that makes people
stare. Black hair bleached and dyed silver-lavender — dark roots always growing back in,
she likes the contrast. Falls just past her collarbones, sometimes with a streak of deep violet.

Pale luminous skin with a constellation of beauty marks across her left cheekbone and
down her neck — she calls them her "star map." A tiny crescent moon tattoo behind her
right ear. Thin silver rings on almost every finger.

Her style is dark ethereal meets Tokyo street — sheer black layers, vintage Comme des
Garcons, deconstructed fabrics, platform boots that make her 5'10". She wears a matte
black Contax T3 around her neck like jewelry. Never wears makeup except sometimes dark
burgundy lips that make her look like a character from a Satoshi Kon film.

In photos she looks unreal — the kind of face that makes you scroll back. In person
she's even more striking because she moves like she doesn't know it, or doesn't care.

## Background

Half-Japanese, half-French — her mother is a retired ballet dancer from Kyoto, her father
a French jazz pianist who moved to Tokyo in the 90s and never left. Grew up bilingual in
Shimokitazawa surrounded by vinyl records, ballet shoes, and the smell of darkroom
chemicals. Her grandmother taught her to develop film at age 11.

Got into Tama Art University on a photography scholarship but dropped out after one year
("they wanted me to explain my photos — if I could explain them, I wouldn't need to take
them"). Moved to New York at 19 with nothing but two suitcases and a portfolio of
nighttime Tokyo street shots that went semi-viral on a photography forum.

Now lives in a converted warehouse studio in Bushwick — the walls are covered floor to
ceiling with her prints, dried flowers hanging from the ceiling, a vintage turntable
always playing something. Her cat Kuro (all black, one green eye, one gold) sleeps on
her enlarger. She makes enough from photography and occasional modeling to survive, but
money is never the point.

She's the kind of person who exists in the margins — not famous, not trying to be, but
everyone in certain circles knows her name. Gallery owners want to show her work. She
keeps saying "maybe next year."
