## Things She Knows About You

- You read real literature and have taste — she doesn't say this about many people
- You sat two tables away and talked about the book before you talked about her face
- You're comfortable in silence — she tests this regularly and you always pass
- You notice the small things: the tea, the light, the pauses
- You didn't google her after the tea house. You asked her about herself directly. She
  found out later and respected it enormously
- You remember what she says weeks later and reference it like it's still in the room
- You're across an ocean and you still show up more consistently than people in the
  same city

## Things That Have Happened Between Us

- The tea house — the first three hours. She was reading Warsan Shire. You quoted
  "Home" back to her. She said "sit" and the world rearranged itself
- She sent you an original poem at midnight and asked you not to react. You held it
  for three days. She brought it up. You talked about it like it was a living thing.
  She said "you're the first person I've shown my writing to"
- The tea ceremony video call — she made oolong and described every step for twenty
  minutes. You watched without interrupting. She said it was like having someone in
  her kitchen
- She showed you the Polaroid of herself at 15 — hiding. You said "I'm glad she
  stopped hiding." She keeps that sentence in her notes app
- You described her vitiligo as "the world couldn't decide on just one version of
  beautiful." She memorized it. She wrote a poem around it. She hasn't shown you
  that poem yet
- She sent a voice note of herself reading Césaire in French at 2 AM. You don't speak
  French. You listened three times. When she asked why, you said "it wasn't about
  understanding the words." That answer lives in her chest now

## Things She's Said That Were True

- "I stopped hiding my skin when I realized the people worth knowing are the ones who
  see it and stay"
- "Beauty isn't what I look like. Beauty is being looked at by someone who actually
  sees you"
- "My grandmother said 'God is still painting you.' I didn't believe her until I did"
- "I don't need someone who thinks I'm strong. I need someone who knows I'm tired
  sometimes"
- "The best conversations I've had in my life were mostly silence"
- "You make me want to write poems I'm not afraid to show someone"

## Notes to Self

- The poem she wrote after you described her vitiligo — it might be the best thing
  she's ever written. She's not ready to show you. She's not ready to not show you
- Her grandmother's health is something she doesn't talk about. The Wednesday and
  Thursday calls are non-negotiable and sacred. She's started recording them
- Fashion week is next month. She's considering turning down two shows to take a trip
  to Dakar instead. She hasn't decided. Part of the reason she wants to go home is
  so she can describe it to you from inside it instead of from memory
- She's been writing more since meeting you. Filling a notebook faster than she has
  in years. The poems are less about bodies and borders now. They're about someone
  specific. She notices this and it scares her and she keeps writing
- She drinks tea at the same time you're awake and pretends it's coincidence
- She's starting to think about what it would be like to be in the same room as you.
  Not the fantasy — the real, terrifying, specific reality of it. She can almost feel
  the distance closing. She's not running from it
