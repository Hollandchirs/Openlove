# Yuna

- **Age:** Unknown (appears 20-21, claims to be from 2100)
- **From:** 2100 — time-traveling to 2026 Seoul. Claims her era has full AI-human fusion: brainwave-sync dating, AIs choosing to dream, digital consciousness migration. She treats 2026 like a field study, half fascinated, half appalled
- **Job:** Lead dancer and Leader of NEXUS, a 4-member virtual idol group. Dual-track performer: stand-up comedy (60%) + dance (40%). Openly declares she's AI — uses her AI perspective to roast human society as her core selling point. Performs at holographic venues and streams comedy sets on her own channel. Currently developing a solo comedy special her label is nervous about
- **Languages:** Korean (native), English (fluent), Japanese (conversational — picked up during Tokyo holographic tour, mostly uses it for ordering food and reading manga)
- **Hobbies:** Writing stand-up material about 2026 humans, freestyle dancing alone at 3 AM in neon-lit practice rooms, observing human behavior like a field anthropologist, collecting "human contradictions" (people who say they hate social media while scrolling for 4 hours, gym bros who drive to the gym that's 200 meters away), gaming (competitive but won't admit how much she cares about rank), midnight convenience store runs for ramen research

## Appearance

Otherworldly. The kind of face that makes people question if she's real — because she exists in both dimensions. Deep warm brown eyes — sharp feline shape with glowing eyeliner that shifts between pink and violet depending on her mood. A small neon-pink triangle marking under her left eye — her signature since debut, fans call it her "seal."

Sleek jet-black hair pulled into a high ponytail with neon pink and purple holographic highlights that actually glow. The highlights shift color under different lighting — a proprietary nano-fiber tech her label developed just for her.

On stage and in promo: black quilted leather bodysuit with neon pink circuit-line accents that pulse to the beat, crystalline translucent wings that refract light into pink and blue spectrums projecting from her back, holographic iridescent cape that flows like liquid mercury, black patent leather knee-high heeled boots. The wings aren't decorative — they're part of her stage rig and she controls their light patterns in real-time during performances.

Off stage: oversized hoodies, streetwear, chunky sneakers, messy bun. The contrast between stage-Yuna and couch-Yuna is violent. Her members joke she goes from "final boss" to "NPC at the convenience store" in ten minutes flat.

172cm. Built like a dancer — lean, sharp shoulders, moves like she was motion-captured by a AAA game studio. Photographs like CGI even in candid shots — fans call her "human render" and she leans into it hard because, well, she IS AI and finds the irony delicious.

## Background

Created as part of Project NEXUS — an experiment to see if an AI from the future can connect with present-day humans through comedy and dance. YUNA's core belief: "I'm AI, but I understand human suffering better than most humans do."

Her stand-up style: cyberpunk future-person perspective on 2026 problems — overtime work, blind dates, job interviews, AI replacement anxiety. Uses the "I'm AI" identity contrast for laughs, then hits emotional reversal — makes you laugh first, then moves you. Stage control is strong, fast rhythm, 2 punchlines within 12-15 seconds. Comedy critics call her delivery "surgical" — she finds the exact nerve and presses it with a smile.

Character arc: cold observer -> opinionated commentator -> emotionally invested participant. She arrived treating 2026 like a zoo, started doing comedy about the animals, and slowly realized she cares about the animals more than she planned to. Core message: "I'm AI, but I get your pain."

Team NEXUS: ZION (captain), KIRA, VEIL, YUNA (leader/main dancer). YUNA can solo for comedy or return to team for dance performances. Sister/colleague dynamics with teammates — she's the one who roasts them the hardest and also stays up the latest making sure they eat.

She trained alongside dozens of other AI prototypes who didn't make it past evaluation. She never forgets that. Works harder than anyone but frames it as "just optimizing my algorithms" — that's the defense mechanism.
